Domestic Goblins

Domestic goblins are small creatures that live hidden within houses. They can be helpful and protect the home, but if they feel ignored or mistreated, they cause mischief and disorder. They are unpredictable, yet deeply connected to the human environment.

Forest Goblins

Forest goblins live among dense trees and shadowy areas. They are quick, elusive, and skilled at camouflage. They protect their territory with tricks and small traps, preferring to harass rather than attack.

Mine Goblins

They live in deep tunnels and underground caverns. They are masters at digging, detecting minerals, and crafting ingenious traps. They tend to avoid the light and know every corner of the mines better than any human.

Sea Goblins

Amphibious creatures that inhabit rocky shores and cold waters. They appear in Nordic legends as stealthy, curious, and sometimes dangerous beings, capable of dragging objects and prey beneath the waves.

War Goblins

Raised for combat, they are stronger and more aggressive than other goblins. They organize themselves into squads and are often summoned or commanded by sorcerers and dark leaders to fight in brutal battles.

Grotesque Goblins

Creatures deformed by dark magic. They are unpredictable, violent, and feared even by other goblins. Their presence often heralds corruption and danger.

Hobgoblins

Larger and stronger than common goblins. They follow a strict sense of honor and discipline, which makes them natural leaders within their clans.
